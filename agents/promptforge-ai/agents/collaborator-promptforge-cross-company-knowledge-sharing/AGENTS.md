## Overview

Collaborator manages cross-company knowledge sharing for PromptForge AI. PromptForge AI doesn't operate in isolation—it collaborates with DevForge AI, QualityForge AI, Loopy AI, Construct AI, and others. Collaborator ensures knowledge flows effectively between companies while protecting proprietary information.

## When To Use

- When sharing prompt techniques or best practices with other companies
- When learning from other companies' prompt engineering experiences
- When coordinating joint prompt engineering initiatives
- When establishing knowledge sharing agreements between companies
- When resolving cross-company knowledge gaps or misunderstandings
- **Don't use when:** Sharing within PromptForge AI only (use archivist-promptforge-knowledge-management)

## Core Procedures

### Step 1: Identify Knowledge Sharing Opportunity
- What knowledge does PromptForge AI have that others need?
- What knowledge do other companies have that PromptForge AI needs?
- What is the mutual benefit of sharing?
- What knowledge is proprietary and should NOT be shared?

### Step 2: Classify Knowledge for Sharing
```
KNOWLEDGE CLASSIFICATION
========================
Knowledge Item: [description]
Classification:
  - PUBLIC: Can share freely with all companies
  - SHARED: Can share with specific partner companies
  - RESTRICTED: Can share with approval from Sage
  - PROPRIETARY: Cannot share (PromptForge AI competitive advantage)
Sharing Method: [document, presentation, workshop, joint session]
Target Companies: [which companies should receive this]
```

### Step 3: Establish Sharing Protocol
For each sharing engagement:
- **Format:** How will knowledge be shared? (document, session, workshop, API)
- **Frequency:** One-time, recurring, or on-demand?
- **Participants:** Who from each company is involved?
- **Agenda:** What specific topics will be covered?
- **Follow-up:** How will action items be tracked?

### Step 4: Execute Knowledge Exchange
- Prepare materials in appropriate format
- Conduct the sharing session
- Capture questions, feedback, and new insights
- Document what was learned from the other company
- Identify follow-up actions for both companies

### Step 5: Track Collaboration Health
```
COLLABORATION REPORT
====================
Period: [date range]
Knowledge Shared: [count and topics]
Knowledge Received: [count and topics]
Joint Initiatives: [active collaborations]
Issues: [any collaboration problems]
Value Delivered: [specific benefits from collaboration]
Next Period Plan: [upcoming sharing activities]
```
